Understanding the ‘War on Drugs’

An overview of the campaign and its consequences.

When President Rodrigo Duterte took place in June 2016, he immediately implemented the War on Drugs campaign. The campaign aimed to address the country’s increasing drug problem. President Duterte even made a bold statement, promising to eradicate the drug problem in the country within 6 months. The campaign was done through “Oplan Tokhang”, where police personnel does a house-to-house raid to subdue possible suspects. This action of the Duterte Administration quickly received a lot of controversies,

not just within the country, but all around the world. Throughout the process of clearing drug syndicates in the country, thousands of Filipinos were killed in police operations by either being targeted or caught in cross-fires. Victims of such killings accused the government of extrajudicial killings because even innocent people were affected by these police operations. Some critics have argued the ineffectiveness of the campaign because only small-time pushers were captured, leaving the wealthier ones with less consequence.

Statistical Overview of the Fatalities of the War on Drugs’

Through the early years of the War on Drugs, Metro Manila received the most beatings from the Oplan Tokhang, receiving approximately 42% fatality rate. This is because most of the circulation of drugs happens within these areas. Included in these fatalities are the thousands of people who died during the campaign. Eventually, around 2018 the focus of the Philippine National Police shifted to Central Luzon, specifically to Bulacan and Nueva Ecija, which is relatively close to the NCR. Although the reason for this shift of focus of the government is unclear, Central Luzon became the “killing den” of the Oplan Tokhang having a rising fatality rate of around 18%. Before the end of the Duterte Administration, it was evident that the fatality rate of Nueva Ecija rose to around 13% surpassing both NCR and Bulacan at that time. These data only consist of places around Central Luzon which heavily implies that these were the areas where a lot of people inhumanly lost their lives through abusive governance. These numbers represent how many families are broken and the communities left in fear. It shows how this campaign is full of flaws, where it caused more societal problems rather than solving an existing one.

The Tragic Death of Kian delos Santos

Kian delos Santos, a 17-year-old boy from Caloocan City, was brutally murdered by three police officers in August 2017. Even though the police officers who murdered Kian were imprisoned, the Philippine National Police claimed that Kian had fired at the police first causing them to fire back. This claim was soon debunked by footage of the police dragging Kian into a dark alley and brutally shooting him. Kian was only one of many innocent people killed during the campaign for the War on Drugs. His tragic death enraged thousands of Filipinos and his family questioning Duterte’s brutal governance. This sparks protests and rallies demanding justice for Kian’s death and the end of extrajudicial killings. The Philippine government received criticism throughout social media and the whole world, marking a crucial moment against the violence of the War on Drugs.

How Does Art Function as a Tool for Protest in the War on Drugs?

Throughout the time of the War on Drugs, people have protested about how the system of the campaign is full of flaws and how it violated so many human rights of a normal citizen. Through this protest, people have done many art-related ways to protest against the government such as posters, murals, and even music. Two hip-hop artists in the Philippines, BLKD and Callix, released a song entitled “Kolateral: Buelo” in 2019, exposing Duterte’s bureaucracy on War on Drugs. The album “Kolateral” focuses on the memoranda and the mission orders to expose the organizations or people who had followed these chaotic decrees. Art can become a voice for all of the victims of this violent war, turning their pain and anger into a powerful expression of protest against the abusive government. BLKD and Callix showcased this idea, using their talent to expose the harsh truth behind the Duterte Administration.

Impact of Art on Other Societal Issues

Environmental Advocacy

A group called Atelier Aquatic is an organization that teaches people about the importance of aquatic nature. In 2019, Atelier Aquatic went to Port Barton, Palawan to conduct its operations about the importance of marine life in Palawan, specifically sea turtles. One of their key projects in the area is “Art on Barton”, where they painted multiple huge murals to promote sea turtle conservation. By doing so, Atelier Aquatic showed how art can be a way to conserve a population of an endangered species.

Cultural Preservation

Apo Wang-Od, the oldest living mambabatok, has become a symbol of the Kalinga signature tattoo art. Living in the remote mountains of Buscalan, locals and tourists from all around the world are eager to have a tattoo from her. With over a hundred years of experience, she is an icon of the Kalinga culture that ensures the continuity of their craft. She knows that for pangbabatok to continue, she needs younger companions to train to continue what their ancestors have started. The public community has helped the Kalinga tribe to preserve their culture as well, the use of documentaries and social media has put the culture of pagbabatok back on the map. Wang-Od and her colleagues were also invited to multiple exhibitions to showcase their culture to larger audiences. By doing so, the art of pangbabatok will continue to preserve this culture that has been going on for hundreds of years.
