Art as a Voice Against Injustice

Impact and Reflection

Use of Art in Addressing Social and Political Issues

Historically, art has played a significant role in addressing social and political injustices, even when not made for protest. Particularly in the Philippines, it has been used as an expression of resistance for the marginalized; such as in the case of protest art during the era of martial law, with which came the emergence of the social realist movement to oppose the Marcos dictatorship. With this, artists created a unique expression of art, deviating from Western aesthetics to explore indigenous and folk art. In the modern day, artists have used their work to challenge injustices and raise social awareness; in particular, addressing extrajudicial killings during Duterte’s “war on drugs” campaign. An example of this would be Resbak! Arts and Resistance to the Drug Killings, an art exhibition that features works of visual art and photography, highlighting the effects of the campaign especially on the vulnerable population.

➢ To Provoke Thought and Elicit Emotion

The ability of art to provoke emotion may be best illustrated in protest art. To effectively address the social and political issues being conveyed to its audience, protest art can be made to use visceral imagery or symbolism to create a strong emotional impact. Carlo Gabuco’s art installation Those Without Names is just one example, creating a deep and immersive experience, to evoke a shocking response among viewers. In this case, Gabuco used actual objects, recordings, and hundreds of photographs with the intent to elicit such reactions. With artworks such as these, the effects of the campaign, particularly the extrajudicial killings, are made more personal to the audience, leading to more awareness of the issues that exist as a result.

➢ Social Media

Social media is a powerful tool in further amplifying the impact of protest art. By exposing more people to such works, and therefore widening the audience to engage in and discuss these works, more opportunities are created for a potential public consciousness that is in favor of change. Gabuco’s installations, for example, reached a wider audience through platforms like Instagram, with viewers commenting on a newfound awareness towards the effects of Duterte’s campaign. However, the nature of social media can make it difficult for meaningful critiques to be recognized, especially when it reaches the wider public, and the art’s message may be diminished or even subverted. An example of this would be Kevin Raymundo’s Tumindig, a viral artwork depicting rows of kneeling fists except for one, raised in objection. The artwork was appropriated by Duterte supporters, superimposing a likeness of Sara Duterte “stomping out” the various figures. This only demonstrates that social media can both empower and potentially neutralize the capability of such art.

Art to Influence Change

Despite this, artists still have massive influence especially in the social media space. The Commission on Human Rights has turned to working with artists to promote human rights issues such as the extrajudicial killings. Angie Umbac, the commission’s advocacy officer, has stated that artists and their work are more effective than conventional means such as discussions and campaigns, in terms of getting the message out to the public. However, the challenge remains to use those reactions and awareness into a more lasting change in the public opinion, which was stated as heavily in favor of the campaign.

“They have a reach bigger than the human rights community. They’re able to go into areas where we are not present. The approach is accessible to the public, noting that people are better able to empathise with artists and their work. It becomes very real to them.”

- Angie Umbac

Relating back to Art in Society

It has always been the role of art in society to reflect experiences and serve as an expression of ideas and beliefs. Even now when systemic injustices and political and social issues persist, art can initiate meaningful dialogue through works that resonate deeply with people, instilling thoughts and emotions that hopefully can be a potential for change. While social media has its limitations, it plays an important role nowadays in allowing a wider discussion and engagement of issues conveyed by protest artworks. Overall, art plays a greater role in society, becoming a tool for raising awareness against the social and political injustices in our country.
